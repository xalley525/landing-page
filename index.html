Polyhedral dice are the best

Whether they're made of acrylic, bone, or metal, polyhedral dice are incredibly satisfying to roll. In fact, it is a scientifically proven fact that rolling dice is good for your health. I firmly believe that one can never have too many dice.

Identifying polyhedral dice

These little pyramids are four sided dice. Thankfully, other shapes are becoming more common, which is an excellent development. These guys are basically caltrops, and they're incredibly painful to step on.

A six sided die is probably the first thing that comes to mind when anyone mentions dice. Most everyone is familiar with them since these guys are used for so many different things.

These are eight sided dice. See how they look like two pyramids stuck together? This makes them easy to identify if you remember that two d4s equals one d8!

These odd looking dice have ten sides. Notice how some of them have two digits to a side? That's because these guys come in two flavors: standard and percentile. The double digits denote the 10s place when rolling for a percent.

Here we have our twelve sided dice. I don't actually have much to say about them. They're not commonly used in the games I play, and they mostly exist to confuse me when I'm reaching for a d20.

Finally, my personal favorite: the twenty sided die. These are the kind of dice I roll most often, so I have more of them than any other type. This comes in very handy when I have a run of bad luck, since I can just swap out my d20 for a new one!
